Geometric Structures on Manifolds

In geometry, if X is a manifold equipped with an action of a topological group G by diffeomorphisms that act analytically, the notion of a structure (G, X) on a topological space allows us to formalize the idea that it is locally isomorphic to X with its G-invariant structure. Spaces with a (G, X)-structure are always manifolds, called (G, X)-manifolds. This concept is often used when G is a Lie group and X a homogeneous space under G.
